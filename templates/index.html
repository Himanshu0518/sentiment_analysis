Understanding Sentiment Scores

Compound Score

The compound score is the overall sentiment score, ranging from -1 (most negative) to +1 (most positive). It combines the effect of all the words in the text and represents the overall sentiment.

Positive (Pos)

The pos score is the probability (between 0 and 1) that the sentiment of the text is positive. A higher value indicates a stronger positive sentiment.

Negative (Neg)

The neg score is the probability (between 0 and 1) that the sentiment is negative. A higher value indicates a stronger negative sentiment.

Neutral (Neu)

The neu score represents the probability (between 0 and 1) that the sentiment is neutral, meaning the text has no strong positive or negative sentiment.
